## Key Roles Defined

### Research Roles

**Chief Scientist**
- PhD from top program (Stanford, MIT, CMU, Berkeley)
- Publications at NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR
- Industry experience at Google Brain, DeepMind, OpenAI, FAIR
- Responsible for: Technical vision, research direction

**Research Scientist**
- PhD in ML/NLP/CV
- First-author papers at top venues
- Responsible for: Novel algorithms, architecture design

**Research Engineer**
- MS/PhD or equivalent experience
- Strong coding + ML fundamentals
- Responsible for: Implement research, run experiments

### Engineering Roles

**ML Engineer (Training)**
- Distributed systems expertise
- PyTorch/JAX proficiency
- Responsible for: Training infrastructure, optimization

**ML Engineer (Inference)**
- Systems programming (C++, CUDA)
- Performance optimization
- Responsible for: Serving, latency, throughput

**Data Engineer**
- Large-scale data processing
- Spark, Dask, Ray expertise
- Responsible for: Data pipelines, curation

**Infrastructure/Platform**
- Kubernetes, cloud platforms
- GPU cluster management
- Responsible for: Compute infrastructure

### Safety & Alignment

**Alignment Researcher**
- RLHF, constitutional AI expertise
- Interpretability research
- Responsible for: Training safe models

**Red Team Lead**
- Adversarial testing
- Security background helpful
- Responsible for: Finding failure modes

## Hiring Strategy

### Where to Find Talent

| Source | Quality | Competition | Approach |
|--------|---------|-------------|----------|
| Big Tech (Google, Meta) | Excellent | Extreme | Poach with equity |
| OpenAI/Anthropic | Best | Impossible | Wait for departures |
| Academia (PhD grads) | High | High | Early engagement |
| Top startups | High | High | Compelling mission |
| Bootcamps/self-taught | Variable | Lower | Technical screens |

### Compensation Strategy

**Cash + Equity Mix:**
- Early stage: Lower cash, higher equity (0.1-2%)
- Growth stage: Market cash, moderate equity
- Late stage: Premium cash, smaller equity

**Competitive Packages (2025):**

| Level | Base | Bonus | Equity (4yr) |
|-------|------|-------|--------------|
| L3/Junior | $200K | 10% | $50-100K |
| L4/Mid | $280K | 15% | $100-200K |
| L5/Senior | $350K | 20% | $200-400K |
| L6/Staff | $450K | 25% | $400-800K |
| L7/Principal | $550K | 30% | $800K-2M |
| Director+ | $600K+ | 40% | $1M-5M |

### Interview Process

1. **Resume Screen** - Publications, experience, projects
2. **Technical Phone** - ML fundamentals, coding
3. **Onsite/Virtual:**
   - System design (ML systems)
   - ML depth (architecture, training)
   - Coding (data structures, algorithms)
   - Research presentation (for researchers)
   - Culture fit

### Retention Strategy

1. **Compelling mission** - Building transformative AI
2. **Autonomy** - Freedom to pursue ideas
3. **Resources** - Compute access, data
4. **Growth** - Learning, conferences, papers
5. **Compensation** - Competitive + upside
6. **Culture** - Collaborative, low politics

## Culture & Values

### Essential Cultural Elements

1. **Safety-first** - Alignment is non-negotiable
2. **Technical excellence** - High bar for quality
3. **Openness** - Share research (where safe)
4. **Velocity** - Move fast, iterate
5. **Collaboration** - No silos
6. **Humility** - We don't have all answers

### Red Flags to Avoid

- **"Move fast and break things"** with AI safety
- **Siloed teams** that don't communicate
- **Publishing pressure** over product
- **Ego-driven research** agenda
- **Ignoring safety** for capabilities
